About Me

I am an incoming M.S. student in Computer Science at Northwestern University, with plans to explore the thesis track. I am currently completing my B.S. in Mathematics with a minor in Computer Science at The University of Texas at Austin. My academic background has trained me to think carefully about algorithms, uncertainty, modeling, and the mathematical structure behind complex systems.

My current research interests lie at the intersection of algorithms, machine learning, reliable AI agents, and human-centered AI. I am especially interested in algorithmic foundations for agent planning, reasoning, tool use, reinforcement learning, graph-based methods, and the evaluation of multi-step decision-making systems. Recently, I have also become interested in embodied AI and robotics, particularly how agents can use memory, emotion, social context, and creative expression to act in more adaptive and human-like ways.

My experience spans risk analytics, machine learning, AI systems, and research. At Toyota Industries Commercial Finance, I worked on credit risk modeling, feature engineering, model validation, backtesting, and dashboard development using SQL, Python, Azure, and Power BI. I have also developed LLM-based financial analysis workflows through FinChat, contributed to scalable AI system architecture through CodeAssist, and applied machine learning and statistical methods in bioinformatics, computer vision, and neuroengineering research.

Across these experiences, I have become especially interested in connecting algorithmic machine learning research with practical AI system building. I care about how models reason, how they fail, how they can be evaluated, and how they can be deployed reliably in real-world settings. Long term, I hope to build AI systems that are not only technically capable, but also interpretable, useful, and meaningfully aligned with human needs.
