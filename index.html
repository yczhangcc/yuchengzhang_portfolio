About Me

Hi, I am Cecilia Zhang. My legal name is Yucheng Zhang, and I usually appear in formal academic and professional records as Yucheng (Cecilia) Zhang.

I am an incoming M.S. student in Computer Science at Northwestern University, starting in Fall 2026, and I plan to explore the thesis track. I am currently completing my B.S. in Mathematics with a minor in Computer Science at The University of Texas at Austin. My academic background has trained me to think carefully about algorithms, uncertainty, modeling, and the mathematical structure behind complex systems.

My research interests are centered on machine learning algorithms, reliable AI agents, ML systems, and human-centered AI. I am especially interested in how AI systems plan, reason, use tools, remember context, and make multi-step decisions. Recently, I have also become interested in embodied AI and robotics, particularly how agents can use memory, emotion, social context, and creative expression to act in more adaptive and human-like ways.

My experience spans risk analytics, machine learning, AI systems, and applied research. At Toyota Industries Commercial Finance, I worked on credit risk modeling, feature engineering, model validation, backtesting, and dashboard development using SQL, Python, Azure, and Power BI. I have also developed LLM-based financial analysis workflows through FinChat, contributed to scalable AI system architecture through CodeAssist, and applied machine learning and statistical methods in bioinformatics, computer vision, and neuroengineering research.

Across these experiences, I have become especially interested in connecting algorithmic machine learning research with practical AI system building. I care about how models reason, where they fail, how they can be evaluated, and how they can be deployed reliably in real-world settings. Long term, I hope to build AI systems that are technically capable, interpretable, useful, and meaningfully aligned with human needs.
